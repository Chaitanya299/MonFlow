SQLCipher & Keystore Implementation Approaches

Ensuring "Extreme Privacy" for the Monflo Vault

Approach 1: Sovereign Key Derivation (Recommended)

The 12-word seed is the master entropy. On first launch, we generate it, then use PBKDF2 (Password-Based Key Derivation Function 2) with hardware-backed salt to derive the SQLCipher passphrase.

  • Mathematical proof of "Extreme Privacy"
  • Same 12 words work for Google Drive backup decryption

Approach 2: Keystore-Primary with Seed Backup

The Keystore generates a random key (unrelated to the seed). The 12-word seed is used only to "Re-generate" the same key on a new phone via a private recovery protocol.

  • More complex logic for key recovery
  • Keystore key is more "isolated" from the seed

My Recommendation

Approach 1: Sovereign Derivation is the cleanest and most "Industry Level" path. It turns the 12-word phrase into the user's ultimate "Financial Identity." If they have the words, they have the vault—regardless of the phone hardware.
