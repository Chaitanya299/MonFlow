Section 1: Key Lifecycle & Derivation

From 12 Words to AES-256 Encryption

The "Master Seed" Flow
1. [BIP-39 Generator] ---> Generates 12 random words (e.g. "apple banana...")
2. [Entropy Source]   ---> 128-bit master entropy derived from words
3. [PBKDF2 Function]  ---> Master Entropy + Hardware Salt (Android ID)
                            + 10,000 iterations
4. [SQLCipher Key]    ---> 256-bit AES passphrase (used to open Room)
Storage Logic

The **128-bit Master Entropy** is stored in the **Android Keystore**, encrypted with a hardware-backed AES key. This allows the background tracking service to "fetch" the entropy and re-derive the SQLCipher key silently.

Does this derivation flow look right?

We are using BIP-39 (the industry standard for crypto wallets) to ensure your 12 words are universally compatible if we ever build a desktop or web version.
